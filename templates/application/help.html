What are Fractal Narratives?

Fractal Narratives represent an inventive new way of creating stories with a potentially unlimited level of detail. In order to understand how this is achieved, one must first understand the principle of fractals themselves. While Wikipedia has an in-depth page regarding fractals, their article focuses on some of the more complex fractal shapes, such as the Mandelbrot set.

A rendering of the Mandelbrot set by Chris Farmer.

Luckily, Fractal Narratives have a far less complicated structure and don't require a degree in Mathematics to create. They operate under one simple principle: any two consecutive fragments of a story can be extended by adding a third optional fragment between them.

On paper this may seem like a trivial distinction; obviously any story can be extended by adding more content. The difference in this case is that the word "optional" should be taken literally. The story needs to make sense regardless of whether on not any given section is included.
